# Employee Handbook — Acme Corp

## Welcome
Welcome to Acme Corp. This handbook outlines our policies, benefits, and expectations. All employees are expected to read and understand these guidelines.

## Working Hours
Standard working hours are 9 AM to 6 PM, Monday to Friday. Flexible hours are available with manager approval. Core collaboration hours are 10 AM to 4 PM and all employees must be available during this window.

## Vacation and PTO Policy
Full-time employees receive 15 days of paid vacation per year in their first two years. After two years, this increases to 20 days per year. Vacation days must be approved by your manager at least one week in advance for periods of 3 or more consecutive days.

Vacation days do not roll over to the next year. Unused vacation is forfeited at year end.

## Sick Leave
Employees receive 10 sick days per year. Sick days can be used for personal illness or to care for an immediate family member. A doctor's note may be required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

## Remote Work Policy
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week. Remote work must be pre-approved by your manager. Employees working remotely must be reachable during core hours and attend all required meetings via video.

## Public Holidays
Employees are entitled to all national public holidays plus 2 floating holidays per year which can be used at the employee's discretion.

## Code of Conduct
All employees are expected to treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect. Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind will not be tolerated and may result in immediate termination.

## Dress Code
Business casual is the standard. Client-facing days and meetings may require business formal attire. When in doubt, err on the side of professional.

## Probation Period
All new employees serve a 90-day probation period. During this time, either party may terminate employment with 1 week's notice. After probation, 4 weeks' notice is required.

## Performance Reviews
Performance reviews are conducted twice per year — in June and December. Reviews include a self-assessment, manager assessment, and peer feedback. Salary adjustments and promotions are typically linked to the December review cycle.

## Promotions and Career Growth
Employees can be promoted based on performance, skills, and availability of roles. Lateral moves are encouraged for career development. Talk to your manager about your career goals during 1:1 meetings.
